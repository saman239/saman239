Introduction

Factory Records, that name has a lot of history behind it, from their Post-Punk Joy Division beginnings to their New Wave Hacienda years, they affected British music so much in the 80s, but today it is scarce. On this page, we will be delving into a deeper look into Factory Records and the late rave scene as a whole they helped crafting, through photography, audio and video. Tony Wilson was the man behind Factory Records, also known as Mr Manchester, he was arguably the most important man in the British music business of the 80s, pioneering the famous post-punk sound of the band of Joy Division, the new wave movement of New Order and the ‘Second Summer of Love’ rave scene with Happy Mondays. All these bands were cultural giants of their respected genre, and behind all of them was Tony Wilson, Factory Records.

To start with, photography, all photos represent something important to the history of Factory Records. I aimed to give my photos a narrative of Factory Records, from their beginnings being a club called ‘The Factory’ in Hulme, to signing Joy Division, opening up the infamous Hacienda with New Order, to a few decades later to Tony Wilsons death. The last image shows what is left, the club FAC 251, all that remains of the Factory Records name. My photos show Factory Records in the modern-day, a very intentional bleak comparison, which was once the breeding ground of musical culture, now apartments, where one of the most influential bands of all time once stood, now just a simple walkway, lost to history.

Next up, the audio piece, a brief touch on the history of Factory Records, the rise and demise. I touch down on all the key moments of their past. I mainly focus on Tony Wilson but do mention other important players that had a hand on the moulding of Factory Records, such as Peter Saville, Peter Hook, etc. My audio mainly focuses’ on the 80s of Factory Records as that was when they were at their peak, making influential moves for the UK music, with their bands and The Hacienda, moves that are still present to this day.

Lastly, my video, a short interview with Peter Summers, my father. Here I put Factory Records in cultural context, wherein the music scene they affected, mainly with the last 80s rave scene. Peter is a perfect candidate for the interview as he grew up around Factory Records and their influence. He was part of the cultural landscape we are discussing, he went to The Hacienda, listened to the Factory Records catalogue of bands. Factory Records had under their belt Happy Mondays, a band who had a major hand in developing the ‘Second Summer of Love’ along with other bands like Primal Scream and The Stone Roses, with this interview I was able to collect first-hand anecdotes of what it was like and what Factory Records did for that movement. I also compared the rave scene of the 80s to the current clubbing scene of now, footage taken in FAC 251 played over Peter describing what his experience was like, to show how similar then and now truly are, with the help of Factory Records.
